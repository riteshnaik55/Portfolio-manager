Shares




Shares are units of equity ownership
interest in a corporation that exist as a
financial asset providing for an equal
distribution in any residual profits, if any are declared, in the form of dividends.


Shareholders may also enjoy capital gains
if the value of the company rises,
or will have to bear loss if value decreases.


Shares represent equity stock in a firm, with the two main types of shares being
common shares and preferred shares.
As a result, "shares" and "stock" are commonly used interchangeably.
