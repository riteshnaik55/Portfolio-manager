Mutual Funds



A mutual fund is a type of financial
vehicle made up of a pool of money
collected from many investors to invest
in securities like stocks, bonds, money
market instruments, and other assets.


Mutual funds are operated by
professional money managers who
allocate the fund's assets and attempt to produce capital gains or
income for the fund's investors.

Mutual funds give small or individual
investors access to professionally managed
portfolios of equities, bonds, and other securities. Each shareholder, therefore, participates proportionally in the gains or losses of the fund.
